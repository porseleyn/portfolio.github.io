Non-Human Cognition

The Non-Human Cognition course focuses on consciousness in all non-human creatures, ranging from animals to robots. With our group we created a poster about elephant communication. It was really fun except that the methods were shit, which we humbly "pointed out" on our poster.

Sciences and Humanities

In the course Sciences and Humanities we were challenged to see the point of view from both the science and humanities side. Somehow this compelled me to write a song about it. If you really need to hear it, go ahead. It is a debate between two friends discussing Popper and Kuhn's views on science.

Digital Drumset

For the course Creative Programming we learned to program in Processing (Java) and PlugData (PureData). At the end of the course we had to create a cool program in both Processing and PlugData. In Processing I made an interactive drumset and in PlugData I made a presentation trainer for shy people that tells you to either speak up or tone it down based on a set volume.

PianoSheetAI

The course Human Computer Interaction and Information Visualisation taught us to develop an interactive platform on our own and user test it. We went through the whole cycle of developing personas, user analyses, sketches, prototypes, scenarios, task analyses and usability specifications. Eventually we came up with PianoSheetAI: a pianosheet generator for musicians with a creative block. We presented it in a very theatrical way and that landed us a prize for best presentation!

A year later we further developed the web app for the course Computational Creativity, but this time with a focus on the co-creative aspect of gaining inspiration.

Bees in a Box

For the course Playful & Creative Science I had the honour of collaborating with my dear friend Annelies. We took an article about bee behaviour during solar eclipses and decided to demonstrate the results of the study in a creative and playful way: through a "peephole" box (in Dutch: kijkdoos)!!

The Night Forest

For the course Sound, Space and Interaction we were taught a lot about sound, acoustics and how to work with PureData. Together with Dom we created an interactive large-scale auditory art installation mimicking a walk in a forest at night. You had to hold a Vive tracker (programmed through Unity and SteamVR) to navigate through three levels of exploration depending on the height of the position: a water, ground, and sky level. You can discover the animals in the forest, but do not get too or the animals get scared and run away to another hiding spot.

Better Together

For the course Artificial Creatures we had to come up with our own artificial creature(s). The one requirement was that it had one human quality: and we chose the need for togetherness. The whole class exhibited their creatures and it was a nice premiere for our little robots! We also had to create our personal pages on a shared website, if you are interested you can take a look at what me and my classmates did.

In September 2024, we were asked to perform with our robots during the Leiden Night of Discoveries for the Critters Exposition. With three big performances and small shows in between we amazed minds of kids and adults alike.

Sneakpeek AI

For the course AI and Society, taught by Zane Kripke, we were challenged to think about the consequences of using AI in our daily lives. As a final assignment, we worked in small groups to apply what we had learned in a specific field of work.

I was in a group with Annelies and Christina, the dream team. We decided to make a Duolingo inspired app about taking away the fear of AI replacing jobs in medicine for medical students.

Essentials for Data Science

In this course we learned to work with data, clean and inspect them, all using Python. We were also taught to work together using git and GitHub. You can find the assignments on my GitHub.
